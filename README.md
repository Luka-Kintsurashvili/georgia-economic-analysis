# Georgia Economic Squeeze Analysis (2012-2024)

## Why I built this
I wanted to investigate why it feels harder to save money in Georgia despite rising average salaries. I decided to use Python to see if the "official" numbers support the reality we feel every day.

## The Goal
To compare **Nominal Wage Growth** against the **Consumer Basket Weights** (how much we are forced to spend on essentials).

## Technical Process
1. **Data Sourcing:** Raw data exported from the National Statistics Office of Georgia (Geostat).
2. **Cleaning:** I used Python to remove percentage symbols and align 12 years of data.
3. **Visualization:** Created a dual-axis chart to show the divergence between earnings and food costs.

## Results
The data shows that food costs are taking up an increasing share of the average Georgian budget (from 30% to 33%+), which explains why higher paychecks aren't leading to a higher quality of life.

**Tools:** Python, Pandas, Matplotlib.
